A Future of Peace

In this moment of rebuilding, the nations of the world must stand united in defending the rights and liberties of every human being. Lasting peace can only be secured through justice, dignity, and mutual respect among all peoples. Let us resolve that future generations shall live free from the fear of war and the oppression of tyranny. Together, we lay the foundation for a world where humanity, cooperation, and universal peace shall endure.

01   General Assembly — Two-Thirds Universal Threshold

All binding decisions require two-thirds approval. Routine resolutions pass by simple majority. This is a mathematically derived threshold that separates genuine international consensus from a manufactured majority. One number will ensure consistency across the board.

02   Veto Power / Security Council — Mandatory Compromise Trigger

A veto with no obligation to propose an alternative is obstruction with legal cover, not diplomacy. Historically, the League of Nations collapsed precisely because powerful states could block action indefinitely with zero accountability. The Mandatory Compromise Trigger removes the incentive to obstruct. It instead requires P5 states to enter the Compromise and Amendment Phase if a resolution passes with a majority but faces a veto.

A Nash Equilibrium is the point where no player can improve their outcome by changing their strategy alone, locking everyone into their best response. Under the current veto system, obstruction costs nothing and blocks everything, making it the permanently dominant strategy for every P5 member. The Mandatory Compromise Trigger introduces cost C into the veto payoff, forcing P5 members into the Compromise and Amendment Phase and making obstruction irrational when C > B − b. The equilibrium shifts from permanent deadlock to negotiation as the dominant strategy.

03   ECOSOC — Coordinating Authority

Post-war reconstruction data from WWI shows that uncoordinated aid distribution produced duplication in some sectors and total gaps in others, resulting in measurable welfare loss. A coordinating body with binding allocation authority eliminates that inefficiency. Advisory recommendations have a documented historical compliance rate near zero when they conflict with national interest.

The Lagrange multiplier tells you the exact cost of every constraint on your budget, so when resources are misallocated you can calculate precisely how much welfare was lost. That is why ECOSOC needs coordinating authority rather than advisory power, because the difference between the two is numerical.

04   Trusteeship & Self-Determination — Transparency with Timeline

Every colonial administration in history that operated without external reporting eventually abused that silence. The absence of transparency guarantees the absence of accountability. Annual benchmarks create a measurable record, and a measurable record creates political cost for non-compliance. Political cost is the only enforcement mechanism that works without military intervention.

05   Regional Alliances — Complementary, not Subordinate

If a Security Council permanent member commits or enables aggression in a region, that same member can veto any Council response. A region with no autonomous defense mechanism is structurally defenseless against the very body meant to protect it. Regional autonomy is what makes global order credible.

06   Sovereignty — We All Deserve to Exist

Every nation that signed the Atlantic Charter did so as a sovereign equal. Permitting intervention in domestic affairs, even with good intentions, creates a legal precedent that the strongest powers will use selectively. Sovereignty is the only principle that protects small nations from becoming permanent subjects of great power preference.

Compromises

The Delegation of Argentina arrives at this table as a nation that believes the strength of any international system is measured by how it treats its smallest members. Buenos Aires is prepared to work directly with P5 nations to find structural solutions that preserve their influence while guaranteeing that influence cannot become paralysis. Argentina does not ask the great powers to surrender their voice. It asks them to use it. What it will not compromise on is the principle that no single nation should have the unilateral power to stop the world from acting when the world has already decided to move. We, as a collective body, are representatives of the human race, and we are all definitionally equal as humans and should thereby recognize each other as such in the pursuit of unity and justice.

Please express your ideas to the Delegation of Argentina during the next unmoderated caucus, your voice deserves to be heard.
